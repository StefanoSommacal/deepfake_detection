What is a deepfake?

The term "deepfake" comes from the underlying technology "deep learning," which is a form of AI. Deep learning algorithms, which teach themselves how to solve problems when given large sets of data, are used to swap faces in video and digital content to make realistic-looking fake media.

What are they used for?

The ability to automatically swap faces to create credible and realistic looking synthetic video has some interesting applications in cinema and gaming. However, this is obviously a dangerous technology with some troubling applications such as the power to create synthetic videos using celebrities faces. A notorious example is this one of Mark Zuckerberg but there are many many more on the Internet.

How do you spot one?

Poor-quality deepfakes are easier to spot. The lip synching might be bad, or the skin tone patchy. There can be flickering around the edges of transposed faces. Hair are particularly hard for deepfakes to render well. Badly rendered jewellery and teeth can also be a giveaway, as can strange lighting effects, such as inconsistent illumination and reflections on the iris.
